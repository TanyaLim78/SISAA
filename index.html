Services and Information about Sexual Assault and Abuse, otherwise known as SISAA, has a mission to educate and inform young people about consent, red flags in relationships, and how to pursue legal action if desired.








Information

❮ ❯



A victim of sexual assault/abuse is NEVER at fault, no matter previous actions or relationships of the victim. No person asks to be sexually assaulted/abused, and choosing to hurt and violate others is a choice made by the perpetrators and is in no way caused by the victim themselves.


Definitions:
  • Sexual Assault/Abuse: Sexual contact or behavior that is unwanted/without explicit consent of the victim including coerced or forced consent such as threats and intiminadation tactics

  • Rape: The FBI defines rape as “penetration, no matter how slight, of the vagina or anus with any body part or object, or oral penetration by a sex organ of another person, without the consent of the victim”

  • Consent: Consent is a verbal affirmation from a victim that must be voluntary, sober, enthusiastic, informed, mutual, honest, and verbal agreement. This agreement cannot be coerced and a person who is intoxicated can NOT legally give consent. This agreement can be changed anytime no matter the relationship between people or if it was given before. Consent can NEVER be implied or assumed

For more information about what to do if you are a victim of sexual assault or how to support victims of sexual assault, check out our support page and visit these sites:
